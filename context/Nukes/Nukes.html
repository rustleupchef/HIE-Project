History Of Fossil Fuels

What is it

Nuclear energy is a form of energy that comes from the energy stored in the core of atoms. There are two ways to release this energy.

  1. Nuclear Fission: Nuclear Fission is the process in which a nucleus splits and in the process a lot of energy is released.
  2. Nuclear Fusion: Nuclear Fusion is the process in which you fuse light nuclei into one big nuclei and in the process a lot of energy is released.

I am going to be talking about mainly just nuclear fission because nuclear fission is more studied and more applicable to this discussion, and nuclear fusion is more of a sci-fi thing at this point because it has only been successfully performed 3 times as far as my knowledge goes, so I don’t think it would be relevant.

Nuclear fission is how we think of nuclear energy. Nuclear fission is performed in these big nuclear reactors. The energy is used to boil water, and the steep is used to turn a giant generator which produces electricity.

Why is it good

The main reason it is good is because of the reliability, clean energy and massive energy production.

Nuclear energy is really reliable because it solves one of the biggest problems where clean energy isn’t alway available, but nuclear energy always is, so that solves one of the biggest issues.

The energy is also very clean because the only product it produces that affects the climate is water vapor which has little to no effect on the environment, so our government is considering it as the #1 alter alternative energy sources for that reason.

It also makes and massive amount of energy
