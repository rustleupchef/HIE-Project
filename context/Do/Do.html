How much the avg person would have to donate to counteract their effect on climate change. I found that the average person worldwide produces 4 tons of carbon dioxide per year. This would mean daily they produce (8000 lb / 365) = 21.91 lbs of carbon dioxide a day. I’ll explain why we need this number later. Then to find how much money the avg person should have to pay daily is a bit more complicated. Let's start out with the total amount of money it would take to counter climate change by my solution. It would take about 15000 nuclear reactors to power the entire world, and a nuclear reactor costs anywhere from 2.7 billion to 9.7 billion dollars lets go with a lower estimate because if reactors are made worldwide we will find ways to make them more efficient, and number I pulled up for “How many reactors would we need to power the world” is for gen III reactors which cost close to the same as gen IV, and gen IV reactors produce more energy. Let's go with 3.5 billion dollars (equivalent to 3.5 * 10^9), so let's multiply the price by the amount needed to power the world. So we get roughly 50 trillion dollars. We want to find how much the world would need to pay individually in 20 years (20 years comes from the idea that 2050 will be the point of no return and we don’t want to wait till the last second to finish the job) to pay this. The equation is equivalent to (50 * 10^12) / ((20 * 365) * (8 * 10^9)) = 0.8561643856. Now we have all the numbers we need. So the idea is that we divide your carbon emissions by the average person's carbon emissions and multiply that by the amount that the average person pays, so that if you are above average in payment you will have to pay more money vise versa. If the number seems high it is likely because we as Americans are the one of the top producers in carbon emissions, so we obviously have to pay a lot more than the average person to counter our carbon emissions.
