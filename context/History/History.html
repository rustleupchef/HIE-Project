History Of Fossil Fuels

Why fossil fuels started

The burning of fossil fuels started during the 1800s where it was used in the heating of homes. Though it really got bad when the steam engine was made because it was so useful. It started replacing all the water powered factories, and the problem was that people didn’t know that coal caused harm to the environment, so they just kept producing.

By the time we started making other solutions and finding out about this problem was around the time that it would be too hard to switch. This was because new solutions weren’t studied enough to be reliable and it would cost too much to switch.

The Issues when switching

  • The most major issue was that most of our economy is based on fossil fuels, so the switch might destroy our economy if done too quickly
  • Some solutions are just not nearly as cheap or profitable as fossil fuels, so many companies prefer fossil fuels
  • Some companies are trying to convince people it's not a big enough of a deal, so that they can keep making money because they are extremely greedy
  • Most renewable energy sources are based on factors of the earth that aren’t consistent which becomes the problem when trying to switch which is that they aren’t reliable enough most of the time.
