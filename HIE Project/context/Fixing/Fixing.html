What are the issue of going nuclear

There are many problems with going nuclear, but I will fire some off really quickly before I go into any solutions to these issues:

  1. Nuclear Waste
  2. Up front cost
  3. Malfunctions are fatal

I want to go a bit in depth of the first 2 issues because they are the ones that have the harder solutions. For nuclear waste we do have a couple of solutions, but they aren’t used worldwide; Those solutions do work when they happen.

For the high up front cost I will be discussing the issue in developing countries because it isn’t as much of a problem for rich and developed countries, so I want to just focus on how poorer countries will make the switch.

I’m not discussing the 3rd problem too much because it has a simple solution which I will be getting into.

How can we fix these issues

I believe that all these issues that I think are the major problems that people are concerned about have a solution. Let's start off with the one that I think has the most simple solution, “Malfunctions are deadly”.

While it is true that malfunctions are deadly, all of the malfunctions that have happened in the past weren’t something that happened out of nowhere, they were things that happened due to people doing stupids things in these situation, For example disobeying many of the safety restrictions (I'm talking about chernobyl).

Now going to talk about the bigger issues; Let’s start off with the cost to switch. I’m not going to see how developed countries/ richer countries are going to build nuclear plants because money isn’t the issue. Now that we will be getting that out of the way I want to talk about carbon tax, and collaborative projects between countries. Carbon tax is for developing countries so we are rewarded by going green, so we are not only encouraging them to go green but also giving them the means to go green. This is in opposition to how carbon tax was thought which was just taking money away from that spread to much carbon which doesn’t necessarily encourage anyone to turn green.

We are also working with other countries in collaboration to help them build their first nuclear power plants. This is because world wide nuclear is seeming the way to go in alternative energy because it's just so good if you can get a nuclear reactor; This is why the U.S is building nuclear power plants in some other countries.

Now I’m going onto the biggest issue which we currently need to solve, “How do we get rid of nuclear waste?”. Many ideas have been thought of for disposal, but none have worked effectively. The best solution has been thought to be deep geological disposal which is just putting nuclear waste in impermeable rock, so there's no leaks. The issue is that it is harder to find a spot that nuclear waste can be disposed of, and to say that we put all reactors here is absurd. What we are instead doing is using Gen IV reactors.

Gen IV reactors

Gen IV reactors are a new type of nuclear reactor that solves almost all the issues of the previous. There is less waste of energy because they can operate at higher temperatures than ever before. They also solve one of the biggest issues which is nuclear waste disposal. Not only can they reuse the nuclear waste that is formed from Gen III reactors, but they can also make nuclear waste that has a much shorter life span, almost half.
